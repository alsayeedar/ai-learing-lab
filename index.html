SAU 48 Statement on Artificial Intelligence (AI)

SAU 48 acknowledges the role of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in enhancing learning, critical thinking, and creativity, while helping to prepare students and our school community for a rapidly changing world increasingly shaped by AI. We see AI as a complement, not a substitute, for human intelligence, fostering original thinking while honoring human ingenuity. Our approach prioritizes ethical, responsible, and transparent use of AI, with a strong commitment to academic integrity, critical evaluation of AI outputs, and protection of student data. We guide our community to use AI to enrich, not replace, authentic learning and understanding.

Key idea: AI can support learning, critical thinking, and creativity, but it should not replace human intelligence, original thinking, academic integrity, or authentic learning.
Academic Integrity and Ethical Use

Students are expected to use technology, including AI, honestly and responsibly. AI use should align with school expectations for integrity.

  • Students should complete their own work unless collaboration or AI support is authorized.
  • AI-generated material used in academic work should be identified and verified.
  • Students must distinguish their own thinking from someone else's ideas or AI-generated content.
  • Misleading or harmful digital content, including manipulated media or deepfakes, is not responsible use.
  • AI should only be used to generate material when authorized by the teacher.
Expectations for AI Use
  • AI may support learning, creativity, organization, and problem solving.
  • AI should not replace original thinking or authentic learning.
  • Learners remain responsible for final work, including AI-supported content.
  • AI tools may generate inaccurate, biased, or misleading information.
  • Learners should verify information using reliable sources.
  • Students must follow teacher expectations for each assignment or activity.
  • Students should be ready to explain, discuss, or demonstrate understanding of submitted work.
  • AI tools are not substitutes for trusted adults, teachers, counselors, family members, or healthy human relationships.
Student Guidelines

Student anchor: AI supports my learning, but does not replace my thinking.

Curious and Self-Directed Learner

  • Approach AI-assisted learning with curiosity, persistence, and independence.
  • Use AI to deepen understanding while recognizing productive struggle matters.

Responsible Digital Citizen

  • Use AI ethically, safely, and with integrity.
  • Understand privacy, bias, academic honesty, and responsible acknowledgment of AI support.
  • Understand that AI is not a friend or trusted adult.

Critical Thinker and Effective Communicator

  • Critically evaluate AI-generated information.
  • Question AI results, analyze sources, and verify accuracy.
  • Use technology to support, not replace, human interaction.

Creative Problem Solver and Innovator

  • Use AI to generate ideas, explore possibilities, and refine solutions.
  • Apply AI knowledge to complex, real-world problems.

Strategic and Independent AI User

  • Understand when AI enhances learning and when it hinders learning.
  • Use AI intentionally while maintaining ownership of thinking and original work.
Family Guidelines

Family anchor: Discuss with your student how AI supports learning, but does not replace thinking.

Curiosity and Independence

  • Encourage curiosity, persistence, and independent thinking.
  • Remind students that challenge and productive struggle support growth.

Safety, Privacy, and Integrity

  • Discuss privacy, bias, academic honesty, and responsible use.
  • Help students understand that AI is not a friend or trusted adult.

Verification

  • Encourage students to question AI results and verify accuracy.
  • Discuss how AI can sound confident even when it is wrong.

Ownership

  • Reinforce that AI can support ideas, but students own the final work.
  • Ask students what they changed, checked, or decided themselves.
Faculty Guidelines

Faculty anchor: Embed the philosophy that AI supports learning, but does not replace thinking.

Model Responsible Use

Show students what ethical, safe, and transparent AI use looks like in learning.

Verify AI Output

Teach students to question AI responses, check accuracy, and use reliable sources.

Clarify Expectations

State when AI is allowed, limited, or not appropriate for each assignment.

Protect Student Privacy

Avoid entering private student information into AI tools or public systems.

Keep Thinking Visible

Design tasks where AI may support the process, but student reasoning remains clear.

AI Levels of Use for Assignments

Use levels clarify how AI may be used on a specific assignment and what documentation is expected.

LevelUseDescriptionDocumentation
0No AI UseThe assignment is completed entirely without AI assistance.No disclosure required; a no-AI pledge may be used.
1AI for PlanningAI may support brainstorming, structure, or idea generation, but no AI content is used in the final submission.AI disclosure and chat link may be required.
2AI for EditingAI may improve clarity or quality of student-created work, but should not create new content.AI disclosure and chat link may be required.
3AI for Defined Task SupportAI may complete specific elements defined by the teacher. Human oversight and evaluation are required.AI-created content should be cited; chat links may be required.
4AI as Supported Co-UseAI may support the assignment more broadly when the teacher allows it. Human creativity, oversight, and evaluation remain necessary.AI use should be cited or disclosed; chat links may be required.
AI Disclosure Statements

Disclosure statements help students communicate clearly with teachers about AI use. They work best when the classroom culture values transparency, reflection, and responsible AI use.

  • Name the AI tool used and include a link when appropriate.
  • Describe the task the tool helped accomplish.
  • List or summarize the specific prompts used.
  • Explain how the output was used in the final submission.
  • Include chat links or prompt history when required.
Sample pattern: I used [tool] to [specific use]. My prompts included [prompt summary]. I used the output to [explain role in final work].

Definitions
  • Artificial Intelligence: digital systems that perform tasks associated with human thinking, such as generating, analyzing, or solving problems.
  • Generative AI: AI that creates new text, images, audio, video, code, or other media from prompts.
  • AI Hallucination: inaccurate, misleading, fabricated, or false information that may appear convincing.
  • Deepfake: digitally altered media that falsely represents what a person said, did, or looked like.
  • Digital Citizenship: responsible, safe, ethical, and respectful use of technology and online resources.
  • Prompt: a question, instruction, or request entered into an AI system.
  • Bias: unfair, inaccurate, or unbalanced information that may appear in AI output or digital content.
